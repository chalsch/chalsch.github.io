Simulation


Simulating data can be a very useful, whether to simulate processes that are difficult to measure or generate dummy datasets to understand and test a model. The following code and presentation are meant to give an introduction to the latter use.


PCA by hand


Principal component analysis is a frequently used analysis in ecology and evolution. Whether the data are of communities or loci, our datasets are often multivariate and we need to reduce them. PCA might be the most common way of doing this. While the code to run one is fairly straighforward, the math behind it can be intimidating, especially when first learning. This lesson decomposes PCA into its component parts and provides a quick introduction to what is going on under the hood.
